DRIVE BELT AUTO-TENSIONER AIR BLEEDING
1. Remove the drive belt (see DRIVE BELT INSPECTION ).
2. Set a long-handled, boxed-end wrench on the drive belt auto-tensioner from the
top of the engine. Apply slow and steady pressure (3 seconds or more to
complete the stroke) to compress the drive belt auto-tensioner. Pull the wrench
back, taking 3 or more seconds to complete the stroke. Do this action a total of
3 times to complete the air bleeding.

3. Install the drive belt (see DRIVE BELT INSPECTION ).
