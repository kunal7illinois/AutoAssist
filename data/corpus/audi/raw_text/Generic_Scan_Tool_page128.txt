Leak checking
– Using a Smoke tester, check the Evaporative Emission
(EVAP) system for leaks.
Note
Always follow the manufacturers directions for the proper instal‐
lation and operation of the smoke tester being used.
If a leak is detected:
– Check the fuel filler cap seal for damage and for proper instal‐
lation. Replace if necessary.
– Check all hose connections of the EVAP system and replace
or repair any leaking lines.
– Check all hose connections of the Fuel Supply system and
replace or repair any leaking lines.
– Remove the rear seat bench. Refer to the Repair Manual .
– Remove the floor cover seal.
– Inspect the seal under the Fuel Delivery Unit (fuel pump) lock‐
ing flange for proper sealing. Also check the fuel line connec‐
tions.
– Repair or replace any damaged component.
If no leaks are found in the EVAP or Fuel Supply System:
– Erase the DTC memory if a code was set. Refer to
⇒ “3.3.4 Diagnostic Mode 04 - Erase DTC Memory”,
page 15 .
– Perform a road test to verify repair.
